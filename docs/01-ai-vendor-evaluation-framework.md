# AI Vendor Evaluation Framework for SMBs

> **The short version:** Most SMBs waste money on AI tools because they skip the evaluation step. Here's a 4-question framework that cuts through the noise and tells you exactly what to buy, build, or ignore.

---

## The Problem: $47K Down the Drain

Gartner's 2025 survey found the average SMB wasted **$47,000 on AI tools they didn't need** — tools that were either wrong for their use case, too complex for their team, or abandoned within 90 days.

The cause isn't bad tools. It's **no evaluation process.**

Enterprise companies have procurement teams. SMBs have a founder with a credit card and FOMO.

---

## The 4-Question Framework

Before you evalute any AI tool, answer these four questions in order:

### Question 1: What specific problem are you solving?

**Wrong answer:** "We need to get into AI."
**Right answer:** "Our customer support team spends 4 hours/day answering the same 10 questions."

**Rule:** If you can't name a specific process, a measurable time/cost, and a desired outcome, you're not ready to evaluate tools. Full stop.

### Question 2: Can you solve this without AI?

**Check this list first:**

- A better spreadsheet
- A simple automation (Zapier, Make)
- An existing feature in software you already pay for
- A temporary hire or contractor

**If yes:** Do that first. AI is not always the answer.

**If no:** Proceed to Question 3.

### Question 3: Does the tool actually solve YOUR problem?

Vendors demo their best case. You need YOUR case.

| What they show you | What you should test |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| "Works with any data" | Upload YOUR messy data |
| "Ready in 5 minutes" | Run it for a week |
| "Enterprise grade" | Ask about SMB pricing |
| "99.9% accuracy" | Test on YOUR edge cases |

**Test rule:** Most vendors offer a 7-14 day trial. Use day 1-3 to set up with real data. Use day 4-7 with your team. If it's not working by day 7, it won't work.

### Question 4: Can your team actually use this?

The best tool is useless if nobody on your team can operate it.

| Team type | Look for |
|-----------|----------|
| No technical staff | No-code tools, drag-and-drop, templates |
| 1-2 technical people | Low-code tools with API access |
| Small IT team | Full platform with admin controls |

**Test:** Give the tool to your least technical team member. If they can't figure it out in 30 minutes, your whole team will struggle.

## The Decision Matrix

| Q1: Clear problem? | Q2: No non-AI fix? | Q3: Works for you? | Q4: Team can use? | Decision |
|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | **Buy** |
| ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ❌ No | **Keep looking** (find a simpler tool) |
| ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ❌ No | — | **Keep looking** (wrong tool for your use case) |
| ✅ Yes | ❌ No | — | — | **Don't buy AI** (fix it non-AI first) |
| ❌ No | — | — | — | **Stop. Define the problem first.** |

**Hard rule:** If you hit a ❌ at any question, stop. Do not pass Go. Do not buy AI.

## The 30-Minute Vendor Screening

When evaluating a specific tool, spend 30 minutes answering these:

```
1. What's their pricing page URL?  →  Can you find it without talking to sales?
2. What's their SMB plan?           →  Or is it "contact us" (read: enterprise-only)?
3. What data do they need?          →  Can you actually give it to them?
4. What's the cancellation policy?  →  Month-to-month or annual lock-in?
5. Who's their current customer?    →  Are any of them your size?
```

If 3+ answers are red flags, move on. There are hundreds of AI tools. Don't get attached to any one.

---

## The Anti-List: What to Ignore

**Don't evaluate these until your fundamentals are solid:**

- AI chatbots that replace your website (fix your products first)
- AI content generators (write your own strategy first)
- AI "co-pilots" for everything (pick one process, not the whole company)
- Enterprise AI platforms with "custom quotes" (they're not for you)
- Any tool that requires a dedicated engineer to maintain

---

## When to Hire a Consultant vs. Buy a Tool

| Scenario | Do this |
|----------|---------|
| One-time AI strategy question | Buy a few hours of consulting |
| Ongoing monitoring and decisions | Subscribe to a service like Keystone |
| Tool selection and setup | Hire a freelancer for the specific integration |
| Building custom AI | Don't. You're an SMB. Buy existing tools. |
